## Summary
Aishwarya Naresh Reganti and Kiriti Badam, AI product practitioners, explain why building AI products is fundamentally different from building traditional software. They highlight the double non-determinism problem -- you cannot predict how users will interact with your product or how the LLM will respond. The episode covers actionable feedback loops, the agency-control tradeoff, and why building step-by-step beats attempting complex agentic systems from day one.

## Key Insight 1
The double non-determinism of AI products changes everything about product development. Unlike traditional software where inputs produce predictable outputs, AI products face uncertainty on both sides: unpredictable user behavior combined with unpredictable model responses. Most teams underestimate this and build as if they are shipping deterministic features, which leads to poor user experiences and unreliable products.

## Key Insight 2
The agency-control tradeoff is a core design decision for every AI product. Every time you hand decision-making capabilities to an agentic system, you relinquish control. The recommendation is to start small and build step-by-step, which forces you to define the actual problem you are solving rather than getting seduced by solution complexity. It is not about being the first to ship an agent but about building the right flywheels for improvement over time.

## Key Insight 3
Actionable feedback loops are essential but widely neglected. Most practitioners agree they matter, yet few companies implement them well. Successful AI product teams embed continuous feedback mechanisms that capture how users interact with AI outputs, what fails, and what succeeds. This data feeds back into model fine-tuning and product iteration, creating a compounding advantage that competitors without these loops cannot match.
