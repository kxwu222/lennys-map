## Summary
An "explain it like I'm 5" guide to 20+ AI terms for product builders who sort-of know what the jargon means but not really. Covers models, LLMs, transformers, training, fine-tuning, RLHF, prompt engineering, RAG, evals, MCP, agents, vibe coding, tokens, hallucinations, AGI, and synthetic data — each with recommended videos and resources for deeper learning.

## Key Insight 1
The five core AI concepts to understand: Pre-training teaches the model general knowledge. Fine-tuning specializes it for specific tasks. RLHF aligns it with human preferences. Prompt engineering crafts better inputs. RAG retrieves external information at runtime to give the model context it wasn't trained on. Hallucinations happen when you don't give the model enough context through RAG.

## Key Insight 2
Transformers (2017, Google) are the breakthrough that made modern AI possible. Instead of reading text word-by-word, they pay attention to all words at once using an "attention" mechanism. This helps models understand relationships between words. They're also highly parallelizable — you can train bigger models by scaling data and compute. Almost every major AI model today is built on transformers.

## Key Insight 3
Agents are AI systems that take actions on your behalf. Unlike chatbots that respond to prompts, agents plan, work step-by-step, and use external tools across apps. They become more "agentic" as they: act proactively, make their own plans, take real-world actions, draw on live data, and create their own feedback loops. MCP (Model Context Protocol) is an open standard that lets agents interact with external tools like calendars, CRMs, and codebases.
