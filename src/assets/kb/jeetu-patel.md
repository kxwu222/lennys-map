## Summary
Jeetu Patel, who manages 30,000 people at Cisco, discusses how to distinguish AI megatrends from hype cycles and how he personally leveraged AI to learn entirely new domains when taking on his expanded role. He shares his philosophy on public debate, preparing for the world six months ahead, and why the survival of humanity may depend on getting AI adoption right given declining birth rates and aging populations.

## Key Insight 1
AI is a megatrend, not a hype cycle, and leaders should not fight it. Patel draws a sharp line between the two: megatrends reshape entire industries and compound over decades, while hype cycles fade. His advice to leaders is to fast-forward six months mentally, prepare your organization for that future state, and make decisions based on where the technology is heading rather than where it is today. At Cisco, this meant transforming a traditional enterprise into an AI-forward company.

## Key Insight 2
Patel challenges a core management principle: the idea that you should praise in public and criticize in private. He argues instead for building enough trust on your team that critique and debate can happen openly in group settings. This transparency accelerates decision-making, reduces back-channel politics, and builds a culture where people are comfortable challenging ideas regardless of hierarchy -- essential when moving fast in AI.

## Key Insight 3
When Patel took on his new role overseeing domains he had no experience in, AI became his essential learning tool. He used AI to rapidly come up to speed on unfamiliar product areas, customer needs, and competitive landscapes. His practical advice: treat AI as a force multiplier for leadership capacity, not just a product feature. Leaders who use AI to expand their own cognitive range will outperform those who only deploy it in products.
