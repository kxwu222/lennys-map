## Summary
Jorge Mazal (former CPO, Duolingo) tells the story of how Duolingo grew DAU 4.5x over four years after growth had stalled at single-digit rates. After two failed attempts — a Gardenscapes-inspired moves counter (neutral impact) and an Uber-style referral program (only 3% lift) — the team built a data-driven growth model that identified current-user retention rate (CURR) as the most powerful lever, with 5x the impact of any other metric.

## Key Insight 1
The growth model segments all users into MECE buckets (new, current, reactivated, resurrected, at-risk WAU/MAU, dormant) with daily retention rates as arrows between them. A sensitivity analysis showed CURR had a compounding effect — current users who stay active return to the same bucket — making it 5x more impactful than the next best lever. This led to creating a dedicated Retention Team focused solely on CURR.

## Key Insight 2
Three growth vectors drove the turnaround: Leaderboards (borrowed from Zynga's FarmVille 2 but adapted — competitors matched by engagement level, not friendship, with automatic opt-in and no extra tasks) increased learning time 17% and tripled highly engaged learners. Push notifications were optimized on timing, templates, and copy with one foundational rule: protect the channel — never increase quantity without CEO approval. Streaks proved users who reach 10 days drop off substantially less.

## Key Insight 3
Key lesson on borrowing features: ask (1) Why does this work in that product? (2) Why might it succeed or fail in our context? (3) What adaptations are needed? The Gardenscapes counter failed because Duolingo lessons lack strategic decision-making. The referral program failed because best users already had Super Duolingo. Use better judgment in adapting when adopting — don't blindly copy what worked elsewhere.
