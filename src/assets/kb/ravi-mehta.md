## Summary
Ravi Mehta, product advisor and former EIR at Reforge, explains why starting AI prototypes with structured JSON data rather than visual design leads to better products faster. He also shares a leadership framework built around a confidence-autonomy matrix that helps product leaders know when to be hands-off versus when to selectively micromanage. The conversation covers practical approaches to AI product development and career growth for PMs.

## Key Insight 1
The JSON-first approach to AI prototyping means defining the data structure and logic before any visual design. By specifying inputs, outputs, and decision logic as structured data, product teams can test whether an AI feature actually solves the user problem before investing in UI. This approach forces clarity on what the AI needs to do, surfaces edge cases early, and makes it easier to iterate on the core logic independently of the presentation layer.

## Key Insight 2
Mehta's leadership matrix has two axes: confidence in direction and team autonomy. The ideal state is high confidence with high autonomy (scalable leadership). When confidence is low, the right move is selective micromanagement -- temporarily getting deeply involved to course-correct, then pulling back. The worst pattern is low confidence with high autonomy, where leaders let teams drift in the wrong direction because they are afraid of being seen as micromanagers.

## Key Insight 3
For product leaders building their career, Mehta emphasizes developing what he calls "product sense" through deliberate practice. This means regularly analyzing products outside your domain, writing down predictions about what will work before seeing results, and building a personal framework library. The compounding effect of these habits over years creates the intuition that distinguishes senior product leaders from those who plateau at mid-level roles.
