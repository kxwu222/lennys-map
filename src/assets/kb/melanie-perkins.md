## Summary
Melanie Perkins, CEO and co-founder of Canva, shares the story of building a $42B company from nothing. She discusses how she pitched over 100 investors who all said no, why she kept going despite sustained rejection, and how Canva's culture of crazy big goals drives the company's ambition. The episode covers strategic decision-making, startup resilience, and how Perkins thinks about vision-setting at massive scale.

## Key Insight 1
Use rejection as a product development tool. Each investor who said no gave Perkins specific feedback: the market was too small, the product was not differentiated enough, or the competitive landscape was too crowded. Rather than being discouraged, she treated each rejection as a prompt to add a new slide to her pitch deck addressing that concern. Over 100 rejections, her pitch and her understanding of the market became extraordinarily refined.

## Key Insight 2
Canva's value of Crazy Big Goals means setting targets where you feel completely inadequate before the goal. That feeling of inadequacy is the signal that the goal is ambitious enough. Perkins maintains a physical wall in her home office with her vision for what the world should look like in 2050. This practice of envisioning a distant, audacious future and working backward from it shapes every strategic decision at Canva, from product roadmap to hiring priorities.

## Key Insight 3
Start by imagining the future you actually want, then work backward to build it. Perkins saw clearly that design tools were too complex for most people and that democratizing visual communication would be transformative. She held that conviction even when the market and investors disagreed. The lesson for founders: if your vision is genuinely strong and user evidence supports it, investor rejection is a test of conviction, not a verdict on viability.
