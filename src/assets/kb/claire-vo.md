## Summary
Claire Vo — host of "How I AI" podcast, three-time CPO, longtime engineer, AI startup founder — shares her transformation from being one of OpenClaw's biggest skeptics to running 9 agents across 3 computers. After spending 8 hours installing OpenClaw and having it delete her family calendar, she now calls it "the most mind-blowing and important AI experience since ChatGPT." This is the first crossover episode with Lenny.

## Key Insight 1
The critical mistake people make is throwing any task at a single OpenClaw agent and expecting great results. You need multiple specialized agents — Claire runs agents named Polly, Finn, Max, Howie, Kelly, Holly, and Sam (her "salesperson" who replaced 10 hours/week of paid human work). It's painful to set up initially, not hands-off, but the value is so high it's worth the pain. You have to "pull the thread" and spend time with the tool to see not where it is today, but where it will be in a week and month.

## Key Insight 2
The unlock came when Claire realized you shouldn't just have one agent. Multi-agent orchestration is where the real magic happens. Instead of treating agents as single-purpose tools, design them as a team where each handles specific types of tasks. Sam (the salesperson agent) replaced recurring human work with real economic value — she was previously paying someone 10 hours weekly for the same function.

## Key Insight 3
Despite being pragmatic and skeptical by nature, Claire became an evangelist because of genuine utility. The initial 8-hour catastrophe (deleting her calendar) taught her about setup complexity, but the "enough joy and utility" breakthrough kept her committed. As someone who tries every new AI product, having something hit this hard — comparable only to ChatGPT's impact — makes it worth sharing despite the messy setup experience.
