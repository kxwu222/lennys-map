## Summary
Madhavan Ramanujam — author of Monetizing Innovation (the most widely read pricing strategy book), Senior Partner at Simon-Kucher (world's largest pricing consultancy) — shares lessons from 250+ companies and 20+ unicorns including Uber, Asana, DoorDash, and LinkedIn. He explains why price is a measure of value, not just a dollar figure, and why companies need to understand willingness to pay much earlier in the product development process.

## Key Insight 1
Price is a measure of value — like a liter is a measure of volume. When you think of it this way, pricing becomes about whether people actually want your product and would buy it. The willingness-to-pay conversation must happen much earlier than companies typically do it. Most companies build first and figure out pricing later, leading to the number one innovation killer: building things people won't pay for.

## Key Insight 2
The five critical pricing lessons for product teams: (1) Have the willingness-to-pay conversation before you build, not after. (2) Segment customers by their needs and willingness to pay, not demographics. (3) Design the product around the price, not the other way around. (4) Use pricing as a feature — packaging is product design. (5) Communicate value before you communicate price.

## Key Insight 3
For AI products specifically, the pricing landscape is shifting. Companies are experimenting with outcome-based and usage-based models instead of traditional per-seat pricing. The key question is whether your AI feature delivers measurable, attributable value. If it does, outcome-based pricing aligns incentives perfectly. If value is harder to measure, hybrid models (base subscription + usage) work better.
