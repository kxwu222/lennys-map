## Summary
Tomer Cohen, Chief Product Officer at LinkedIn, discusses the platform's significant turnaround and its strategy of transforming product managers into AI-powered "full stack builders." The conversation covers how LinkedIn repositioned its feed as a place for professional content that matters, why starting from objectives rather than technology is essential for AI adoption, and how the PM role is evolving as AI tools enable individuals to do what previously required entire teams.

## Key Insight 1
LinkedIn's turnaround started with redefining the feed's purpose: it is not a springboard for other products or an upsell mechanism, but a place where people who matter talk about things you care about professionally. This clarity of vision guided every product decision. Cohen emphasizes starting from the premise of what the platform's potential is -- a platform for economic opportunity built on a strong social graph -- and working backwards.

## Key Insight 2
The shift to AI-first thinking at LinkedIn was deliberate and counterintuitive. Instead of asking what cool things they could do with AI technology, Cohen challenged teams to let go of what they had already built, return to their core objectives, and ask how AI could achieve those objectives better. This reframing prevented the common trap of bolting AI features onto existing products without rethinking the fundamental approach.

## Key Insight 3
The "full stack builder" concept reflects a broader industry shift where PMs are expected to prototype, build, and ship using AI tools rather than solely writing specs and coordinating. Cohen sees a future where the boundaries between product management, design, and engineering blur significantly, and the most effective PMs are those who can leverage AI to move from idea to working product with minimal handoffs.
