## Summary
Jason Cohen, a two-time unicorn founder, presents a systematic five-question framework for diagnosing why a product has stopped growing. The framework covers logo retention, pricing, net revenue retention (NRR), marketing channels, and target market -- in that specific order. Cohen argues that most founders jump to marketing or new features when growth stalls, but the root cause is almost always upstream in retention or pricing.

## Key Insight 1
The framework must be followed in order because each question builds on the previous one. First, check logo retention: are customers actually staying? If they are leaving, no amount of marketing will fix growth. Second, examine pricing: are you capturing appropriate value? Many companies underprice, which limits both revenue and the quality of customers they attract. Only after retention and pricing are healthy should you look at expansion revenue through NRR.

## Key Insight 2
Net revenue retention (NRR) is the most powerful growth lever that many companies neglect. If your NRR is above 100%, your existing customer base grows automatically without acquiring a single new customer. Cohen emphasizes that improving NRR through upsells, expansion, and usage-based pricing is often far more efficient than acquiring new logos. Companies with strong NRR can survive marketing downturns; companies without it are on a treadmill.

## Key Insight 3
The final two questions -- marketing channels and target market -- are where most founders mistakenly start. Cohen advises that when growth stalls, founders should resist the urge to try new channels or pivot their market. Instead, go back to first principles: talk to your best customers (the ones who stay and expand), understand why they chose you, and double down on finding more people exactly like them. The target market question is really about focus and saying no to customers who are not a great fit.
