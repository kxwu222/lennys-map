## Summary
Lazar Jovanovic is a full-time professional vibe coder at Lovable, building both internal tools and customer-facing products purely using AI without a traditional coding background. He represents a new AI-era job title and workflow: someone who translates product vision into working software by collaborating with AI coding tools rather than writing code manually. The conversation explores his daily process, the tools he uses, and what this means for the future of building software.

## Key Insight 1
Vibe coding is not casual tinkering -- Jovanovic treats it as a professional discipline with its own workflow and quality standards. His process involves clearly articulating requirements, iterating through AI-generated versions, testing thoroughly, and knowing when to push back on the AI's suggestions. The key skill is not coding ability but product thinking: understanding what to build, for whom, and how to evaluate whether the output is good enough to ship.

## Key Insight 2
The rise of professional vibe coding fundamentally changes who can build software. Jovanovic had no traditional engineering background, yet he produces tools used by real customers and internal teams at Lovable. This democratization means that domain experts -- people who deeply understand a problem space -- can now build solutions directly rather than waiting in a product queue or writing specs for engineers. The bottleneck shifts from technical talent to problem clarity and taste.

## Key Insight 3
Jovanovic's practical workflow tips include: starting with a clear description of the end state rather than step-by-step instructions, using AI tools to generate a first version quickly then iterating on specifics, and maintaining a tight feedback loop between building and testing. He emphasizes that the biggest mistake non-technical builders make is accepting the first output without pushing for improvements -- the value comes from the iteration, not the initial generation.
