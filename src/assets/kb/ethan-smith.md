## Summary
Ethan Smith, CEO of Graphite and Lenny's go-to SEO expert, explains AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — how to show up in LLM answers from ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity. As people shift from Google to AI for answers, a new channel is emerging. Webflow saw a 6x conversion rate difference between LLM traffic and Google Search traffic. ChatGPT is already driving more traffic to Lenny's Newsletter than Twitter.

## Key Insight 1
AEO differs fundamentally from traditional SEO. In Google, you win by getting your blue link to rank first. In LLMs, the model summarizes many citations — so you need to get mentioned as many times as possible across different sources. Reddit threads, YouTube videos, blog mentions, and third-party articles all count as citations that influence LLM recommendations.

## Key Insight 2
The significant misinformation about AEO is that Google Search is dying. In reality, Google's slice of the pie stays the same — the pie just gets bigger. AEO is an additional channel, not a replacement. Early-stage companies can win quickly because you can get mentioned by a citation tomorrow and start showing up in LLM answers immediately, without years of domain authority building.

## Key Insight 3
The leads from answer engines are significantly more valuable than traditional search — Webflow's 6x conversion rate difference proves this. People asking LLMs have higher intent and get more specific recommendations. The tactical playbook: maximize your mentions across authoritative sources, ensure your product appears in comparison content, and create content that LLMs naturally want to cite.
