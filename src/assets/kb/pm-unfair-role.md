## Summary
Tal Raviv (ex-Patreon, Riverside, Wix) shares seven productivity tactics for PMs facing "the great flattening" — rising expectations for fewer ICs to own larger product areas. Instead of waiting for fairness, super-IC PMs build their own systems, methodologies, and tools. The strategies range from eliminating post-meeting tasks to using AI for writing, managing Slack boundaries, and protecting cognitive freshness.

## Key Insight 1
Do action items live in meetings while screensharing — write Slack summaries, create Jira tickets, schedule follow-ups, and ping colleagues all before the meeting ends. Colleagues appreciate it because you're bringing clarity and alignment in real time. Replace half your meetings with "59-second Looms" — treat async video as a core PM communication skill, pitching brevity and time ROI.

## Key Insight 2
Cultivate a self-reliant team by empowering teammates as discipline experts. Remind teams that incredible products have been built without a PM, and your best initiatives were never your ideas. Shower teammates with positivity when they do "PM work" (QA doing analysis, designers going to stakeholders). Redirect DMs to public channels — the team in public threads is half a day ahead of the team waiting on your DMs.

## Key Insight 3
Use AI for writing but not for reading. LLMs excel when PMs provide tons of context via speech-to-text (Whisper, BetterDictation). Use AI to draft PRDs, user stories, and blog posts from your dictated thoughts. But avoid letting AI summarize customer insights — your competitive advantage is staying in the weeds with raw data. Also: PM your own brain's freshness by disconnecting with the same discipline you bring to work.
