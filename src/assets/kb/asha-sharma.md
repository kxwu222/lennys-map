## Summary
Asha Sharma, CVP of AI Platform at Microsoft, shares how 80,000 companies are building with AI on Microsoft's platform and where things are headed. She introduces the concept of products shifting from static artifacts to living organisms that learn and improve with every interaction. As the marginal cost of good output approaches zero, agents will scale productivity and fundamentally reshape organizational structures, making traditional org charts obsolete.

## Key Insight 1
Products are evolving from artifacts to organisms. Because AI models are now highly effective, companies can tune them to specific outcomes, creating products that think, live, and learn. This continuous improvement through user interaction becomes a company's new intellectual property. The shift means product teams must think in terms of adaptive systems rather than static feature sets.

## Key Insight 2
Microsoft sees AI adoption moving through distinct seasons: season one was prototyping, followed by a focus on foundational and reasoning models, and now we are entering the agentic era. Planning roadmaps becomes chaotic when new model capabilities arrive frequently. Teams must adopt flexible, season-based planning rather than rigid annual roadmaps to keep pace with rapid model improvements.

## Key Insight 3
As agents handle more tasks, the org chart becomes a work chart. Layers of management shrink because the way you scale to meet exponential demand for productivity is with agents, not more employees. Leaders should prepare for a future where agents outnumber human employees and organizational design is oriented around workflows and outcomes rather than reporting hierarchies.
