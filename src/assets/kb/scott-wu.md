## Summary
Scott Wu, CEO of Cognition, explains how Devin — the world's first autonomous AI software engineer — works as an actual remote engineer you chat with via Slack. Cognition's 15-person engineering team uses up to 5 Devins each, with Devin merging several hundred PRs into production monthly. About 25% of their PRs are committed by Devin today, expected to exceed 50% by year's end. They went through 8 pivots before landing on this path.

## Key Insight 1
AI is the biggest technology shift of our lives, but unlike previous revolutions (PC, internet, mobile), there's no hardware distribution constraint — the market isn't growing steadily as phones ship. AI capability is improving exponentially, meaning companies building for AI see sudden step-function changes rather than gradual adoption curves. This makes timing and speed of iteration critical.

## Key Insight 2
The practical reality of working with AI engineers: Devin acts as a teammate you communicate with in natural language through Slack. Each human engineer on the Cognition team works with up to 5 Devins simultaneously, effectively multiplying their output. The role of an engineer shifts from coder to architect — defining what needs to be built, reviewing output, and handling the hardest judgment calls.

## Key Insight 3
Scott believes AI tools like Devin will lead to more engineer hiring, not less. As the cost of building software drops, the total amount of software the world wants to build increases dramatically. The bottleneck shifts from "can we code this?" to "should we build this and how should it work?" — making product judgment and architectural thinking the most valuable engineering skills.
