## Summary
Brendan Foody, CEO of Mercor — the fastest company in history to go from $1M to $500M revenue (17 months), youngest unicorn founder ever — explains why we're entering the "era of evals." Mercor helps AI labs and companies hire domain experts to train and evaluate their models. They work with six of the Magnificent 7 and all top five AI labs, with over 1,600% net retention and zero customer churn.

## Key Insight 1
If the model is the product, then the eval is the PRD. Researchers run dozens of daily experiments making small improvements on eval sets. Reinforcement learning is so effective that once they have an eval, they can climb it rapidly — look at how fast Olympiad Math and SWE-bench were saturated. The barrier to applying AI agents across the entire economy is measuring success — writing evals for everything we want agents to do.

## Key Insight 2
Mercor discovered that the real bottleneck for AI labs isn't compute or algorithms — it's access to domain experts who can evaluate model outputs in specialized fields. This created an entirely new category of jobs: people who write evals, provide expert feedback, and help labs understand what "good" looks like in areas from medicine to law to coding.

## Key Insight 3
For enterprises, the core question is: how can you build systematic ways to measure how well AI performs your specific tasks? Companies doing evals well build feedback loops where human expert judgment trains better models, which enables more automation. Brendan doesn't think we'll see AGI or superintelligence anytime soon, but the economic value of better evals is already enormous.
