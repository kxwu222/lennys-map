## Summary
Hamel Husain and Shreya Shankar, creators of the most popular AI evaluation course, explain why evals have become the critical skill for anyone building AI products. They break down what evals are, why most teams get them wrong, and how product builders (not just ML engineers) can design evaluation systems that actually improve AI product quality. The conversation covers practical frameworks for measuring AI output quality at every stage of development.

## Key Insight 1
Most AI product teams fail because they skip rigorous evaluation and rely on vibes-based testing. Effective evals require defining clear success criteria before building, creating diverse test cases that cover edge cases and failure modes, and building automated evaluation pipelines that run continuously. The key insight is that evals are a product skill, not just an engineering skill -- product managers who understand user needs are often better at designing meaningful evaluation criteria than engineers.

## Key Insight 2
There are multiple levels of AI evaluation: unit-level evals test individual model outputs against criteria, integration evals test how AI components work together in a pipeline, and system-level evals measure end-to-end user outcomes. Teams should start with simple human evaluation rubrics, then progressively automate using LLM-as-judge approaches. The most common mistake is jumping to automated evals without first establishing reliable human baselines.

## Key Insight 3
For product builders getting started with evals, the practical playbook is: (1) collect real user queries and edge cases from production or customer interviews, (2) define what "good" looks like with specific, measurable criteria tied to user outcomes, (3) build a small but representative test set of 50-100 examples, (4) run evaluations before every model or prompt change, and (5) treat your eval suite as a living product that evolves with user needs. Teams that invest in evals early ship better AI products faster.
