## Summary
Amir Klein shares how he uses ChatGPT Projects as a "second brain" for product management at monday.com. Instead of trying to hold all product context in your head — Slack threads, Notion pages, research data, past decisions — you dump everything into a ChatGPT Project and let it store, synthesize, and retrieve context for you. Your judgment and taste still do the real work; AI just clears the mental overhead.

## Key Insight 1
The workflow has three steps: (1) Create its personality — write instructions that define how your AI partner should think, challenge you, and behave. (2) Feed it information — export everything as PDFs and upload: Slack channels, docs, dashboards, research transcripts, CSVs. Everything is essentially text. (3) Let it cook — use it for sign-up forms, prototypes via Lovable prompts, and tailored communications for different audiences.

## Key Insight 2
The biggest unlock is that your second brain removes the "context reload cost." When you need to communicate the same initiative to marketing, leadership, enablement, and designers — each needing a different angle — the AI already holds all the context and can produce tailored versions instantly. Tasks that used to eat half a day take minutes.

## Key Insight 3
Context is the raw material PMs think with. When it lives only in your head, output is limited by working memory. When it sits outside your head, intact and retrievable, your thinking expands. At monday.com, the second brain surfaced a key insight within minutes: users weren't blocked by capability but by confidence. That insight unlocked the product direction they shipped.
