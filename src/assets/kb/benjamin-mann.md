## Summary
Benjamin Mann, co-founder of Anthropic and architect of GPT-3 at OpenAI, discusses AGI timelines, AI safety, and what the future looks like. He puts the 50th percentile chance of superintelligence at 2028, explains why he left OpenAI over safety concerns, and shares how Anthropic operationalizes alignment into everything they build. He also addresses the $100M talent war and why Anthropic has been less affected by poaching.

## Key Insight 1
The 50th percentile chance of hitting superintelligence is around 2028. Once we reach it, it will be too late to align the models — safety work must happen now. The existential risk forecast for an extremely bad outcome is somewhere between 0 and 10%. Scaling laws have not slowed down and are in fact accelerating, with the current bottlenecks being compute and high-quality data rather than theoretical limits.

## Key Insight 2
Anthropic retains talent despite $100M offers from Meta because mission-oriented people see a clear difference: the best case at Meta is making money, while the best case at Anthropic is affecting the future of humanity. Ben left OpenAI because safety wasn't the top priority there. The case for safety has gotten more concrete — superintelligence is about keeping "God in a box."

## Key Insight 3
Looking 20 years out past the singularity, it's hard to imagine capitalism will look anything like it does today. No one is immune to job replacement — it's coming for all of us eventually. Ben encourages his own kids to focus on adaptability and human connection rather than specific technical skills that may become obsolete.
