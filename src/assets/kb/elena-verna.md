## Summary
Elena Verna returns for her fourth appearance on Lenny's Podcast with updated perspectives on growth strategy, product-led growth, and pricing. Known for her deep expertise across B2B and B2C growth, Verna shares evolved frameworks on what the head of growth role should look like, how to think about metrics that matter, and how pricing strategy connects to sustainable product-led growth in the current market.

## Key Insight 1
The head of growth role needs to be redefined. Verna argues that many companies misunderstand what growth leadership means, treating it as a marketing function when it should be a cross-functional discipline that sits at the intersection of product, data, and go-to-market. Effective growth leaders own metrics end-to-end and have the authority to influence product decisions, not just acquisition channels.

## Key Insight 2
Product-led growth requires making your product genuinely lovable before layering on growth mechanics. Verna emphasizes that PLG is not a hack or shortcut -- it only works when the underlying product delivers clear value quickly. Companies that try to engineer viral loops or freemium models on top of a mediocre product waste resources. The sequence matters: build something people love first, then optimize how they discover and adopt it.

## Key Insight 3
Pricing is the most underleveraged growth lever. Verna shares frameworks for thinking about pricing as a strategic tool rather than a one-time decision. She recommends revisiting pricing regularly, segmenting by willingness to pay, and using pricing tiers to align value delivery with revenue capture. Many companies leave significant revenue on the table by setting prices once and never iterating, even as their product and market evolve.
