## Summary
Dan Shipper, co-founder and CEO of Every, runs an AI-native media company with 5 products, 7-figure revenue, and 100% AI-written code. He shares how a small team leverages AI tools to build and ship products at a pace that would normally require much larger teams. The conversation covers the practical reality of building a business where AI does the coding while humans provide taste, judgment, and direction.

## Key Insight 1
Every runs as a fully AI-native startup — all production code is written by AI. The team uses AI coding tools not as assistants but as primary builders, with humans providing direction, quality control, and product judgment. This approach allows a tiny team to maintain multiple revenue-generating products simultaneously.

## Key Insight 2
The key mental shift is treating AI as a capable junior developer rather than an autocomplete tool. You provide context, direction, and constraints — then review and iterate. The 100% AI-written code claim isn't a gimmick; it reflects a genuine workflow where human value comes from knowing what to build, not how to write each line.

## Key Insight 3
Building AI-native means rethinking team structure entirely. Instead of hiring engineers, Every invests in people with strong product sense who can direct AI tools effectively. This model suggests a future where company headcount stays small while output scales dramatically — the team ships at a velocity that would traditionally require 5-10x more people.
