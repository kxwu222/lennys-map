## Summary
Data from TrueUp shows the PM job market is recovering strongly. Open PM roles are up 53.6% from the 2023 bottom — over 6,000 globally — and engineering roles are at their highest since late 2022. AI PM roles are exploding with 688 open positions. Layoffs are slowing, capital investment is up 29% YoY, and remote work is settling around a 20% baseline.

## Key Insight 1
AI roles are the biggest growth story: open AI PM roles have skyrocketed, including positions at AI-native companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Scale) and AI-specific roles at non-AI companies (Figma, Salesforce). Almost a third of all AI-related roles globally are based in the Bay Area. The rise of "product engineer" roles (415 open) is an emerging trend worth watching.

## Key Insight 2
The Bay Area continues to dominate opportunity — nearly 20% of all PM roles and over 17% of engineering roles are based there, and the share is growing. Berlin and Austin entered the top 10 PM locations, replacing Boston and LA. For AI jobs specifically, Bay Area concentration is even higher at roughly one-third of all open roles.

## Key Insight 3
Remote work is declining toward a new baseline. At its peak in late 2022, about 35% of PM roles were remote-friendly; now only 23% offer remote options. The trend is identical for engineering and design roles. Meanwhile, the overall outlook is optimistic: more open roles, fewer layoffs, and increasing capital investment signal a healthier market ahead.
