## Summary
Eoghan McCabe, founder and CEO of Intercom, describes how the company was approaching zero net new ARR before making a radical pivot to AI. When ChatGPT launched, McCabe declared wartime and bet the company on building Fin, an AI agent for customer support. The result: Fin is on track to surpass $100 million ARR in less than three quarters. This episode covers the cultural transformation, hard decisions, and pricing strategies behind one of SaaS's most dramatic turnarounds.

## Key Insight 1
McCabe declared Intercom a wartime company immediately after seeing ChatGPT's capabilities. He rewrote company values specifically designed to cut out ineffective parts of the organization. His view: AI will disrupt in the most aggressive, violent ways, and if you are not actively pursuing it, you are about to be pushed out entirely. The CEO must be willing to make brave, hard decisions and own the results.

## Key Insight 2
Intercom shifted to outcome-based pricing for Fin, charging per resolution rather than per seat. This aligned the product's value directly with what customers actually care about: problems solved. The move away from traditional SaaS seat-based pricing reflects a broader industry trend where AI agents deliver measurable outcomes, making usage or resolution-based pricing the natural model.

## Key Insight 3
The turnaround required founder mode at full intensity. McCabe personally drove the AI strategy, restarted the culture, and was willing to lose people who could not adapt. His practical advice for leaders trying to make a similar shift: if the organization is not moving, you may need to go hardcore founder mode. Greatness requires a CEO who owns the transformation end to end.
