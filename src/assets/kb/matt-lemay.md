## Summary
Matt LeMay, product management consultant and author, shares the single most important question product managers should ask to save their careers and build stakeholder trust. The conversation dives into why PMs fail not because of bad strategy or poor execution, but because of misalignment with stakeholders on what success looks like. LeMay provides practical frameworks for communication, alignment, and navigating the ambiguity that defines the PM role.

## Key Insight 1
The career-saving question is a variation of asking stakeholders what success looks like before diving into solutions. Most PMs rush to build and ship without confirming that leadership, engineering, and design share the same definition of done. By explicitly surfacing expectations upfront, PMs avoid the trap of delivering something technically correct but organizationally misaligned.

## Key Insight 2
LeMay argues that overcommunication is the most undervalued PM skill. Product managers who proactively share context, surface risks early, and keep stakeholders informed build trust that compounds over time. The PMs who fail often do excellent analytical work but operate in a communication vacuum, leaving stakeholders to fill gaps with their own assumptions and anxieties.

## Key Insight 3
A practical takeaway: treat every project kickoff as an alignment exercise rather than a planning exercise. Before writing a spec or prioritizing a backlog, run a lightweight alignment session where each stakeholder states what they believe the goal is. The gaps revealed in that conversation are more valuable than any roadmap, because they expose the misunderstandings that derail projects downstream.
