## Summary
Chip Conley — Burning Man founding board member, creator of the second-largest US boutique hotel chain at 26, Brian Chesky's personal mentor at Airbnb, and founder of the Modern Elder Academy (MEA) — discusses thriving in tech as you age, what he learned working with Chesky, building great company culture, and how a near-death experience changed his life's trajectory.

## Key Insight 1
When older brains connect the dots and younger team members bring speed and focus, the combination is brilliant. At Airbnb, Chip's role as "Modern Elder" proved that wisdom and experience complement startup energy. People won't notice your wrinkles as much as they'll notice your energy. The key is being curious, not just experienced.

## Key Insight 2
Midlife is the chrysalis, not the crisis — it's the caterpillar-to-butterfly transformation. Chip is happier at 64 than at 47 during his "flatline experience." This insight led him to create MEA, the world's first midlife wisdom school, with campuses in Baja and Santa Fe. The program helps people navigate career transitions and find renewed purpose.

## Key Insight 3
Success in tech at any age comes from staying adaptable and showing up with energy rather than entitlement. Chip's experience mentoring Brian Chesky at Airbnb showed that the most valuable thing an experienced person can offer isn't answers — it's pattern recognition, emotional steadiness during crises, and the ability to ask the right questions when stakes are highest.
