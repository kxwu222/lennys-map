## Summary
Bret Taylor — co-creator of Google Maps, inventor of the Like button, former CTO of Facebook, co-CEO of Salesforce, OpenAI board chairman, and now CEO of Sierra — shares lessons from one of the most extraordinary careers in tech. He explains why the whole market is moving toward agents and outcomes-based pricing, and why his biggest failure at Google led directly to Google Maps.

## Key Insight 1
The whole market is going toward agents and outcomes-based pricing — it's "so obviously the correct way to build and sell software." Productivity software is incredibly hard to sell (he learned this the hard way). Agents that deliver measurable outcomes solve this fundamental go-to-market problem. Sierra, his current company, builds AI agents for enterprise customer service and sales.

## Key Insight 2
After a tough product review with Marissa Mayer and Larry Page for Google Local, Bret was given a second shot that became Google Maps — reaching 10 million users on day one. The lesson: massive failures don't end careers if you learn from them. It's empowering to know you can succeed in spite of failure. His mindset: wake up every morning asking "what is the most impactful thing I could do today?"

## Key Insight 3
The skills and mindsets that enabled Bret's success across so many different roles — product manager, CTO, CEO, board chairman — come down to impact orientation, adaptability, and the willingness to take on roles where you're uncomfortable. As coding tools get more powerful, the value shifts from writing code to understanding what to build and why.
