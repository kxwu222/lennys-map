## Summary
Howie Liu, co-founder and CEO of Airtable (13 years in), shares how he restructured the company into "fast thinking" and "slow thinking" groups to accelerate AI investment. He discusses the trend of CEOs becoming ICs again, the specific skills PMs/engineers/designers need for the AI era, and his litmus test: if you were founding a new company with the same mission, would you use a fully AI-native approach? If you can't, find a buyer.

## Key Insight 1
Howie's restructuring split Airtable into two groups: a fast-thinking group that experiments rapidly with AI features, and a slow-thinking group that maintains the core platform. This structure allowed AI investments to significantly accelerate without destabilizing the product millions of customers depend on. The key was giving permission to move at different speeds.

## Key Insight 2
Individual attitude matters more than role when it comes to AI productivity. The biggest advantage goes to anyone who can cross over into adjacent roles — a PM who can prototype, a designer who understands data, an engineer with product instinct. PMs need to become "hybrid PM-prototypers" with good design sensibilities. Howie encourages people to cancel all meetings for a week just to explore AI tools.

## Key Insight 3
The CEO-as-IC trend is real and necessary. Howie himself got back into the product, building things and leading initiatives directly. His existential test for any company: if you were founding from scratch today with the same mission, how would you execute using a fully AI-native approach? If the answer is "radically differently," you need to transform now — or find a buyer and restart.
