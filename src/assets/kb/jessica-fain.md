## Summary
Jessica Fain, who has led product and design at Webflow and Slack, argues that influence is the single most important skill in the AI era because it requires understanding people, building trust, and navigating organizational dynamics -- none of which AI can replicate. She shares frameworks for how product leaders can become more effective by mastering persuasion, stakeholder alignment, and cross-functional leadership rather than focusing solely on technical output.

## Key Insight 1
Influence is fundamentally about understanding what motivates different people and tailoring your communication to meet them where they are. Fain distinguishes between authority (positional power) and influence (earned trust). In product management especially, you rarely have direct authority over engineers, designers, or executives, so influence becomes your primary lever for getting things done and driving alignment across teams.

## Key Insight 2
AI can generate documents, analyze data, and even draft strategies, but it cannot read a room, sense political dynamics, or build the kind of trust that comes from repeated human interaction. Fain argues that as AI handles more of the "production" work in product and design, the people who differentiate themselves will be those who can align stakeholders, resolve conflicts, and inspire teams -- skills that require emotional intelligence and lived experience.

## Key Insight 3
Fain offers a practical framework for building influence: start by deeply understanding the goals and incentives of your key stakeholders, then find ways to make their priorities part of your narrative. Rather than pitching what you want, frame proposals in terms of what matters to the person across the table. This approach works at every level, from convincing an engineer to prioritize a feature to getting executive buy-in for a strategic shift.
