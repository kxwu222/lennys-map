## Summary
Nick Turley, Head of ChatGPT at OpenAI, offers a rare inside look at building and scaling the fastest-growing product in history. The conversation covers how ChatGPT went from an internal research demo to a product used by hundreds of millions, the product decisions that shaped its trajectory, and how OpenAI thinks about shipping velocity, user intuition, and maintaining quality at unprecedented scale.

## Key Insight 1
ChatGPT's explosive growth was not the result of a traditional go-to-market strategy. The product launched as a research preview with minimal marketing. The key insight is that when a product delivers genuinely new capabilities that people immediately understand and value, organic growth can be extraordinarily powerful. The lesson for product builders: invest in making the core experience undeniably useful before investing in distribution.

## Key Insight 2
OpenAI's product culture prioritizes shipping speed and learning from real users over perfection. Turley describes an environment where teams ship rapidly, measure actual usage patterns, and iterate based on what people do rather than what they say. This approach is especially critical for AI products where user behavior with new capabilities is inherently unpredictable and cannot be fully anticipated through research alone.

## Key Insight 3
Building product taste and intuition matters more than frameworks at the frontier. When you are creating an entirely new product category, there are no established playbooks. Turley's practical advice: surround yourself with users, develop strong opinions loosely held, and be willing to make bold product bets. The best AI product decisions come from deeply understanding how people actually interact with the technology.
