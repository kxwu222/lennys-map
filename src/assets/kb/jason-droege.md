## Summary
Jason Droege, CEO of Scale AI, discusses how the company provides foundational training data to every major AI lab and powers enterprise AI adoption. He shares insights from scaling Scale AI, the massive Meta deal, and his prior experience co-founding Scour with Travis Kalanick and building Uber Eats from idea to $20 billion in revenue. The conversation covers the gap between AI hype and on-the-ground enterprise reality.

## Key Insight 1
Enterprise AI deployments take 6 to 12 months to become truly robust enough to automate important processes. The general trend in AI is moving from models that know things to models that do things. The next major shift is agents that make decisions on behalf of users. Like any major tech revolution, the headlines tell one story while the ground-level work of implementation is painstaking and incremental.

## Key Insight 2
The nature of training data has evolved dramatically. Eighteen months ago, data labeling meant comparing short stories. Now it requires extraordinary domain experts evaluating complex multi-step reasoning, code generation, and specialized tasks. Scale AI's role has expanded from simple labeling to building comprehensive evaluation systems that frontier labs depend on to measure and improve model capabilities.

## Key Insight 3
Droege's career pattern reveals a repeatable playbook: identify massive market opportunities where incumbents are under-investing, then build operational infrastructure at scale. He applied this at Uber Eats (food delivery logistics) and now at Scale AI (AI data infrastructure). His advice for operators is to focus on the unglamorous infrastructure layer that enables breakthrough products rather than chasing the products themselves.
