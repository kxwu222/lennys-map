## Summary
Dr. Fei-Fei Li, known as the Godmother of AI for her foundational work on ImageNet that helped spark the deep learning revolution, discusses why world models and spatial intelligence represent the next major frontier in AI. She shares her perspective on AI's impact on jobs, the role of robotics, and why the technology is ultimately a double-edged sword whose outcome depends on human choices. The conversation bridges technical AI research with practical implications for product builders.

## Key Insight 1
The next frontier of AI is spatial intelligence and world models. Current AI systems primarily process language and 2D images, but the physical world operates in three dimensions with complex spatial relationships. World models that understand physics, space, and causality will unlock entirely new categories of applications, from robotics to augmented reality. This shift from language-first to world-understanding AI represents the most significant upcoming paradigm change.

## Key Insight 2
Technology is a net positive for humanity, but every technology is a double-edged sword. AI will have real impacts on jobs and people, but its effects are determined by human choices, not by the technology itself. Li emphasizes that there is nothing artificial about AI: it is inspired by people, created by people, and most importantly, it impacts people. Product builders should design with this human-centered perspective rather than treating AI as autonomous force.

## Key Insight 3
Li's breakthrough insight with ImageNet was recognizing that training machines to think required giving them the same volume of data that humans absorb as children. She chose visual intelligence because humans are deeply visual animals. This principle of training AI through the lens of human learning remains relevant: the best AI products succeed by understanding how humans actually perceive and interact with the world, then building systems that complement those natural patterns.
