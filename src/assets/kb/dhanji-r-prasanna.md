## Summary
Dhanji R. Prasanna, CTO of Block (formerly Square), explains how he is transforming a 4,000-engineer organization into one of the most AI-native large companies in the world. He shares concrete productivity data, describes how their internal AI agent Goose is changing engineering workflows, and addresses the debate about whether AI productivity gains are real or overhyped. The conversation provides a practical blueprint for enterprise AI adoption at scale.

## Key Insight 1
Engineering teams that are highly AI-forward report saving 8 to 10 hours per week, and this is the worst it will ever be. Prasanna frames the current moment as the baseline, not the ceiling. The value of AI tools changes daily as models improve, so organizations need to ride the wave continuously rather than evaluate AI once and move on. Leaders who wait for proof of ROI before adopting will find themselves permanently behind.

## Key Insight 2
The most surprising beneficiaries of AI coding tools are non-technical people. At Block, employees outside engineering are using AI agents and programming tools to build things that previously required engineering support. Meanwhile, one engineer has Goose monitoring his Slack conversations, and the agent proactively builds features discussed in chat and opens pull requests. This blurs the line between who builds software and who requests it.

## Key Insight 3
Enterprise AI transformation requires cultural change, not just tool deployment. Prasanna's approach at Block involved creating an environment where experimentation with AI is expected and supported, measuring adoption and productivity systematically, and sharing success stories across the organization. The technical implementation is straightforward compared to the leadership challenge of shifting mindsets across thousands of engineers.
