## Summary
Jen Abel, co-founder of Jellyfish and GM of Enterprise at State Affairs, delivers a tactical playbook for founder-led enterprise sales covering the $1M to $10M ARR stage. She explains why early-stage founders fail when they apply late-stage sales advice, how to craft messaging that breaks through inbox noise, and why vulnerability and honesty about your startup stage is a competitive advantage rather than a weakness. This is a follow-up to her earlier episode covering zero to $1M ARR.

## Key Insight 1
Sell the alpha, not the feature. Early-stage founders are the product: they have studied and experienced something most of the market has not yet seen or visualized. Instead of pitching a feature list, founders should sell their unique insight into where the market is heading. The alpha is your unfair knowledge advantage, the future state you can see that your buyers cannot. This creates urgency and positions you as a thought leader rather than a vendor.

## Key Insight 2
Cold outreach works when messaging uses shock value or counterintuitive framing. In a world where buyers receive thousands of SaaS pitches, generic problem-solution messaging gets ignored. Focus on something surprising or contrary to conventional wisdom that compels the reader to keep going. When a problem is truly being felt by the market, people will respond and get on a call, but only if your message stands out from the noise.

## Key Insight 3
Be vulnerable and honest about being an early-stage startup. Many founders try to appear bigger and more established than they are, which backfires with sophisticated enterprise buyers. Instead, lean into your stage: tell prospects you are early, that you are building something new, and that you want their input. This transparency builds trust and creates a collaborative dynamic where the buyer feels invested in your success rather than being sold to.
