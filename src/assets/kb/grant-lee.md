## Summary
Grant Lee, CEO of Gamma, tells the story of how his AI-native presentation tool went from being called the dumbest idea investors had heard to reaching $100M ARR. The episode covers Gamma's product-led growth strategy, how they found product-market fit in an unlikely category, and the strategic decisions that drove explosive growth. Lee shares the lessons of building in a space where incumbents like PowerPoint and Google Slides seemed unbeatable.

## Key Insight 1
Gamma succeeded by reframing the problem. Rather than building a better slide editor to compete with PowerPoint, they built an AI-first content creation tool that generates beautiful presentations from ideas and outlines. The insight was that people hate making presentations but need to communicate visually. By removing the design burden entirely and letting AI handle layout and aesthetics, Gamma unlocked a market of users who previously avoided presentations or suffered through creating them.

## Key Insight 2
Product-led growth powered Gamma's rise. The product spread virally because every presentation created and shared became a distribution channel. Recipients saw the Gamma branding, experienced the quality, and signed up themselves. This organic growth loop meant Gamma spent very little on traditional marketing while acquiring users at massive scale. The lesson: build distribution into the product experience itself rather than bolting it on afterward.

## Key Insight 3
When investors and industry experts call your idea dumb, that can be a signal of opportunity rather than a warning. The best startup ideas often look bad initially because they challenge existing assumptions about what is possible or desirable. Lee persisted because he had direct evidence from user behavior that people wanted a fundamentally different approach to presentations. Founders should trust user signal over expert opinion when the two conflict.
